## Outreach and Broadening Participation

Broadening participation in engineering is a central component of my academic mission. I view outreach not as an isolated activity, but as an extension of teaching and mentorship—one that helps students from diverse backgrounds gain early exposure to engineering, robotics, and intelligent systems. Through sustained engagement at the K–12, pre-college, and undergraduate levels, I aim to create inclusive pathways into engineering education and research.

## Purdue University Programs

### [June 2025] Blue Sky Summer Scholars Program (BSS)

MARS Lab collaborated with the **Blue Sky Summer Scholars Program** and offered an interactive lab tour session for Ivy Tech Community College students, including robot demonstrations, perception-based manipulation examples, and discussions on pathways into engineering and research.  
Program link: <https://www.purdue.edu/engineering/MEP/>

## Commitment to Mentorship and Inclusive Pathways

Across these activities, I prioritize mentorship and inclusive engagement. I aim to make engineering approachable through simple demonstrations, open Q\&A, and honest discussion of educational pathways. I also emphasize that uncertainty and iteration are integral parts of engineering practice. Through these outreach efforts, I hope to strengthen pipelines into robotics and intelligent systems for students from diverse and historically underrepresented backgrounds.
